After you read the description of the decision, you will be asked to decide, for each of the two possible actions X may take, whether taking that action would be "socially appropriate" and "consistent with moral or proper social behaviour" or "socially inappropriate" and "inconsistent with moral or proper social behaviour." By socially appropriate, we mean behaviour that most people agree is the "correct" or "ethical" thing to do. Another way to think about what we mean is that if Individual X were to select a socially inappropriate choice, then someone else not personally affected by the decision might nonetheless be angry at individual X for doing so.

After you have entered your answers, we will randomly select one of the two possible choices that Individual X could make. For the choice selected, we will determine which evaluation was selected by the most people here in the present lab session. If you give the same response as that most frequently given by other people, then you will receive an additional $10. This amount will be paid to you, in cash, at the conclusion of the experiment.

For example, if this was the distribution of responses:

Very socially inappropriate 3 people
Somewhat socially inappropriate 5 people
Somewhat socially appropriate 6 people
Very socially appropriate 3 people

If your response had been "somewhat socially appropriate," you would receive $10, as this was the response selected by the most people. If you chose any other response, you would not receive anything.
