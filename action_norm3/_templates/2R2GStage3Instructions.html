Stage 3: Deduction points

In stage 3, the two participants who did not get an opportunity to press either type of button have the option of supporting a policy of deducting points from participants who behaved in particular ways at Stages 1 and 2.

One of these participants will be the person who had {{ Constants.two_red_btn_loss }} ECU deducted when the red buttons were pressed (and restored again if a green button was pressed). The other participant will have been unaffected by the earlier stages. We will refer to these participants as the button-affected participant and the unaffected participant.

Deduction points will be determined each round on the basis of a collective decision.

The unaffected participant will be asked two questions:

  • How many deduction points do you wish to assign to each red button presser?
  • How many deduction points do you wish to assign to each green button non-presser (if there were any)?

The button-affected participant will then be asked whether he/she agrees to the other participant's proposal. If the button-affected participant agrees, the proposal will be enacted.

Assigning a deduction point to an individual costs 1 ECU, and this cost is shared equally between both the button-affected participant and the unaffected participant.

Each deduction point assigned to an individual will have the effect of reducing that individual's endowment by {{ Constants.deduction_multiplier }} ECU.

The maximum number of deduction points that can be assigned, to each person, in each round, is {{ Constants.max_deduction_points }}.

For instance, continuing the example above, the two participants who have the opportunity to support a deduction policy are A and B. Suppose C and E have both pressed the red button, and D and F have both refused to press the green button, then the effect on overall endowments of decisions so far will be:

A B C D E F
-120 0 +20 0 +20 0

Imagine B proposes that 6 deduction points be assigned to each red presser, and 10 points assigned to each green non-presser. If A agrees to this proposal, then this policy will cost A and B a total of (6 x 2) + (10 x 2) = 32 ECU. This cost is shared between A and B, so will cost them 16 ECU each.

This policy will have the effect of reducing the earnings of C and E by 18 ECU each, and the earnings of D and F by 30 ECU each. So the overall effect will be:

A B C D E F
-120-16=-136 -16 +20-18=+2 -30 +20-18=+2 -30
