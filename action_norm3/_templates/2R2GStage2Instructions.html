Stage 2: The green buttons

This stage arises when, on the previous stage, both red button participants pressed the red button. If both buttons were pressed, a third participant would have had their endowment reduced. If only one red button was pressed, this stage will be skipped, and you will proceed directly to stage 3.

In this stage, two of the other three participants will be chosen at random to have the opportunity to press a green button.

The green buttons do the "reverse" of the red button. If one of the green buttons is pressed, it will:

  • reduce the endowment of whoever presses it by {{ Constants.one_green_btn_loss }} ECU, and
  • increase the endowment of whoever lost ECU in the previous stage by {{ Constants.one_green_btn_gain }} ECU, returning them to their original endowment.

If both of the green buttons are pressed, it will:

  • reduce the endowment of each person who presses by {{ Constants.two_green_btn_loss }} ECU, and
  • increase the endowment of whoever lost ECU in the previous stage by {{ Constants.two_green_btn_gain }} ECU, returning them to their original endowment.

To illustrate, suppose that persons D and F are the ones chosen to have the opportunity to press the green button.

If both D and F do not press the button, then everyone's earnings from the earlier stage will be unchanged. So the changes from initial endowments will be:

A B C D E F
-120 0 +20 0 +20 0

If D does press the button, but F does not, the overall effect will be:

A B C D E F
0 0 +20 -40 +20 0

If both D and F press their green buttons, the overall effect will be:

A B C D E F
0 0 +20 -20 +20 -20

After the decision has been made whether or not to press the green button, participants enter the final stage of the round, Stage 3.
