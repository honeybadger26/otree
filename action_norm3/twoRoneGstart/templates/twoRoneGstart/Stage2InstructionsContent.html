Stage 2: The green button

This stage arises when, on the previous stage, both red button participants pressed the red button. If both buttons were pressed, a third participant would have had their endowment reduced. If only one red button was pressed, this stage will be skipped, and you will proceed directly to stage 3.

In this stage, one of the other two participants will be chosen at random to have the opportunity to press a green button.

The green button does the “reverse” of the red button. If pressed, it will:

  • reduce the endowment of whoever presses it by 40 ECU, and
  • increase the endowment of whoever lost ECU in the previous stage by 120 ECU, returning them to their original endowment.

To illustrate, suppose that person D is the one chosen to have the opportunity to press the green button.

If D does not press the button, then everyoneʼs earnings from the earlier stage will be unchanged. So the changes from initial endowments will be:

A B C D E
-120 0 +20 0 +20

If D does press the button, the overall effect will be:

A B C D E
0 0 +20 -40 +20

After the decision has been made whether or not to press the green button, participants enter the final stage of the round, Stage 3.
