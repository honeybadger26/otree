Stage 2: The green button

This stage arises when, on the previous stage, one participant pressed the red button, and another participant had their endowment reduced.

In this stage, two of the other three participants will be chosen at random to have the opportunity to press a green button.

The green button does the “reverse” of the red button. If at least one of the two individuals presses the button, it will:

  • reduce the endowment of both green button individuals by 40 ECU, and
  • increase the endowment of whoever lost ECU in the previous stage by 120 ECU, returning them to their original endowment.

If both people press the green button, it will still increase the endowment of the person who lost 120 ECU, but the cost of pressing will now be shared equally (20 ECU each) between the two people who press.

If no one presses the green button, endowments will be unchanged.

To illustrate, suppose that persons B and D are one chosen to have the opportunity to press the green button.

If neither B nor D presses the button, then everyoneʼs earnings from the earlier stage will be unchanged.

A B C D E
-120 0 +40 0 0

If only B chooses to press the button, the effect on earnings will be:

A B C D E
-120+120=0 -40 +40 0 0

If both B and D press the button, the effect will be:

A B C D E
-120+120=0 -20 +40 -20 0

After the decision has been made whether or not to press the green button, participants enter the final stage of the round, Stage 3.
