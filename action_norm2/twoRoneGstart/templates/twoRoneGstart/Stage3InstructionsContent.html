Stage 3: Deduction points

In stage 3, the two participants who did not get an opportunity to press either type of button have the option of supporting a policy of deducting points from participants who behaved in particular ways at Stages 1 and 2

One of these participants will be the person who had 120 ECU deducted when the red buttons were pressed (and restored again if the green button was pressed). The other participant will have been unaffected by the earlier stages. We will refer to these participants as the button-affected participant and the unaffected participant.

Deduction points will be determined each round on the basis of a collective decision.

The unaffected participant will be asked two questions:

  • How many deduction points do you wish to assign to the red button presser?
  • How many deduction points do you wish to assign to each green button non-presser (if there was one)?

The button-affected participant will then be asked whether he/she agrees to the other participantʼs proposal. If the button-affected participant agrees, the proposal will be enacted.

Assigning a deduction point to an individual costs 1 ECU, and this cost is shared equally between both the button-affected participant and the unaffected participant.

Each deduction point assigned to an individual will have the effect of reducing that individualʼs endowment by 3 ECU.

The maximum number of deduction points that can be assigned, in total, on each round, is 30.

For instance, continuing the example above, the two participants who have the opportunity to support a deduction policy are A and B, because C and E had the opportunity to press the red button, and D had the opportunity to press the green button. Suppose C and E have both pressed the red button, and D has refused to press the green button, then the endowments will be:

A B C D E
80 200 240 240 240

Imagine B proposes that 6 deduction points be assigned to each red presser, and 10 points assigned to the green non-presser. If A agrees to this proposal, then this policy will cost A and B a total of (6 x 2) + 10 = 22 ECU. This cost is shared between A and B, so will cost them 11 ECU each.

This policy will have the effect of reducing the earnings of C and E by 18 ECU each, and the earnings of D by 30 ECU. So the final outcome will be:

A B C D E
69 69 222 210 222

If A rejected Bʼs proposal, then no deduction would occur, and the endowments would be unchanged.
