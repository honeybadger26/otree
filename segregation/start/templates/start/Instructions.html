At the beginning of the experiment, the participants are divided into populations of eight. Each participant interacts only with the participants within his/her own population.

Before starting this task, every participant is awarded 600 points for participating.

Each population of eight is divided into two groups: The Reds and the Blues. Each group consists of four members.

This task consists of 15 periods. You are in the same group with the same people for all 15 periods.

Each member in the Reds will be identified as RID# and each in the Blues will be identified as BID#. For example, if a participant with ID #3 is assigned to the Reds (or the Blues), he/she will be identified as R3 (or B3) during the experiment. All participants keep their identifiers for all 15 periods.

There are two towns: Red town and Blue town.

For the first 3 periods, individuals reside in the town that matches their color. So all red individuals will reside in Red town, and all blue individuals will reside in Blue town. After these 3 periods are over, individuals can move between towns as often as they like.

For the remaining periods (starting from period 4):

First, you will decide whether to stay in the same town or to move to the other town. Which town you reside in will affect your earnings, as follows.

Each period, each individual earns additional points, which are distributed to everyone who resides in the same town. Red individuals earn an additional 50 points every period. Blue individuals earn an additional 150 points every period.

After all participants have chosen where to reside, the points that have been earned by all the individuals in a town are equally split between all the residents of that town.

After each period, you will be shown the entire population of the town you have moved into, and how much each person will earn as a result.

Finally, you will decide whether or how much to reduce the earnings of other persons in your current town, by distributing deduction points.
